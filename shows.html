SHOWS

PEOPLE, PLACES, AND THINGS

The play follows Emma, an actress struggling with addiction, as she checks into rehab after a public breakdown. Defiant and skeptical of treatment, she battles with withdrawal, trauma, and the blurred line between her roles on stage and in life. Through intense therapy, Emma confronts her painful past and the "People, Places, and Things" that trigger her, in a gripping exploration of addiction, identity, and recovery.

THE GOOD PLACE

Eleanor Shellstrop finds herself in an idyllic afterlife, "The Good Place," only to realize she doesn't belong there due to a clerical error- her life on earth was anything but "good". Desperate to avoid being sent to The Bad Place, she scrambles to reform her ways, teaming up with an eccentric group of fellow residents. Together, they face absurd challenges, unexpected twists, and profound questions about what it truly means to be a good person.

WHO'S AFRAID OF VIRGINIA WOOLF?

George and Martha, a middle-aged couple, invite a younger couple, Nick and Honey, over for late-night drinks after a univeristy event. What starts as casual conversation quickly spirals into a brutal, emotional battle. As George and Martha draw their guests into their intense, often cruel games. As the evening unravels, long-hidden truths emerge, exposing the raw, fractured layers of their marriage. Through sharp dialogue and dark humor, the play delves into themes of illusion, reality, and the complexities of human relationships.
